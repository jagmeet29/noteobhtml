    • P = Principal (the initial sum of money)

  • Calculation Basis: Simple Interest is always calculated on the original principal amount. Unlike compound interest, you do not earn interest on the accumulated interest.
  • Consistency: A key characteristic of SI is that the interest earned remains the same for every year, provided the principal and rate do not change. For example, if the interest for the first year is $100, it will be $100 for the second year, third year, and so on.

Instructor's Tip: If a problem does not specify whether the interest is simple or compound, you should assume it is Simple Interest.
